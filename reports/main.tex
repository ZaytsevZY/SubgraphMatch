\begin{abstract}
This paper presents a reproduction study of GuP, an exact subgraph matching method based on guard-based pruning. We implemented a correctness-oriented baseline matcher and extended it with a \emph{GUP-lite} design that supports reservation-based pruning, nogood-based pruning, metric collection, and filtering-strategy ablation. The study includes a literature taxonomy, a clear statement of reproduction scope, experiments on three successful datasets (Yeast, Human, and HPRD), a small optional extension on query-result reuse, and a time-boxed boundary evaluation on WordNet and Patents. Overall, reservation-based pruning is already effective on several real workloads and is the main source of measurable search-space reduction in our prototype, whereas the current nogood mechanism remains conservative and the Python implementation still shows a clear scalability boundary.
\end{abstract}
